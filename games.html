DAMPED MASSES

Instructions:

This is an illustration of free vibration of damped systems.

  • Drag and drop to move and release the masses.
  • Drag between masses to move both the adjacent masses.
  • Drag on the sides to move all masses simultaneously.
  • Also try swiping across the masses like you would a piano.
  • Have fun! :-)

P.S. The undamped system should never stop.



Theory: What is the damping ratio?

For a spring mass system with a mass m and stiffness k, there exists a minimum value of viscous damping given by $ c_c=2 \sqrt{mk} $ such that releasing the mass from a distance with no initial velocity would result in no overshoot and the quickest return to the mean position. This ratio of the actual damping to the critical value of damping $ c_c $ is given by the damping ratio $ \zeta $. While most books would show the response-versus-time plots, this demo allows one to play with spring mass systems with various damping ratios to get a feel for the system behaviour.
