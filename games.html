DAMPED MASSES

Instructions:

This is an illustration of free vibration of damped systems. A variety of systems with the damping ratios ranging from zero (undamped), between zero and one (underdamped), exactly one (critically damped), to greater than one (overdamped).

How to play:

  • Drag the masses to move them.
  • Drag between masses to move both the adjacent masses.
  • Drag on the sides to move all masses simultaneously.
  • Also try swiping across the masses like you would a piano.
  • Have fun! :-)

P.S. The undamped system should never stop (unless you make it to).

Theory: What is damping ratio?

For every spring mass system with a mass m and stiffness k, there exists a critical value of damping given by [[ c_c=2*sqrt(mk)]]. Damping ratio is the ratio of the actual damping factor c to the critical damping factor.
