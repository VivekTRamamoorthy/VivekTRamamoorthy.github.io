Hi there!

As part of the Computation Optimisation and Learning Lab (COL) at UoN, I explore areas at the intersection of artificial intelligence (metaheuristics) and mechanical design (topology optimisation in acoustic porous materials).

I am fascinated by science and nature. I love learning new and interesting concepts. If you do too, you might like a compilation of concepts that I found fascinating in the Teaching section. If you think I should check out something else, let me know ! :-)

I find true happiness in seeing the enlightenment in one's face when they learn and truly understand something. I have figured that I am cut out for teaching and I aspire to be a really good teacher some day.

I also have some cool demos. Check them out too. Have fun!

    Concepts that fascinate me

    These are some of the concepts that I found intriguing. It is always interesting to ask people what concepts fascinate them the most. I always learn something new. Every person has their own list of things that fascinate them. Here is mine. If you aren't familiar with any, do look them up. If you think I have missed something, let me know. :-)

    1. Euler's identity: \( e^{i\pi} +1 =0 \)
    2. Calculus: From \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) to \( i\hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{\partial^2 \Psi}{\partial x^2} + V \Psi \)
    3. Principle of conservation of momentum - The fact that it holds true from photons to blackholes
    4. Gauss' divergence theorem
    5. Fourier series and transforms, the FFT algorithm.
    6. Eigen value analysis - natural vibrating frequencies of objects
    7. Modal superposition technique - finding the response of multi dof systems to vibrations
    8. Mohr's circle - How a piece of chalk breaks under tension and torsion
    9. Gyroscopic effect
    10. Coriolis effect
    11. Castigliano's theorems
    12. Cauchy's integral theorem
    13. Planck's black body radiation (The birth of quantum mechanics)
    14. Michelson and Morley experiment -> Speed of light being constant for all observers
    15. Special relativity and general relativity
    16. Principle of equivalence
    17. Young's double slit experiment -> Many-worlds interpretation?
    18. Superfluid helium
    19. Cryogenic liquefaction cycles - The art of liquifying gases
    20. Monte Carlo method - Simple and powerful.
    21. Fermat's last theorem
    22. Neural Networks: emotion recognition
    23. Sound absorption mechanisms in porous media
    24. Structural topology optimisation
    25. Antichess: (weakly solved) 1.e3 wins for White
