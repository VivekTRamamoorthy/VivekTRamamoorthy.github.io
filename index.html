Hi there!

As part of the Computation Optimisation and Learning Lab (COL) at UoN, I explore areas at the interface of artificial intelligence (metaheuristics, machine learning) and mechanical design (topology optimisation in acoustic porous materials).

I am fascinated by science and nature. I love learning new and interesting concepts. If you do too, you might like a compilation of concepts that I found fascinating in the Teaching section. If you think I should check out something else, let me know ! :-)

I find true happiness in seeing the enlightenment in one's face when they learn and truly understand something. I have figured that I am cut out for teaching and I aspire to be a really good teacher some day.

I also have some cool demos. Check them out too. Have fun!

    Concepts that fascinate me

    These are some of the concepts that I found intriguing. It is always interesting to ask people what concepts fascinate them the most. I always learn something new. Every person has their own list of things that fascinate them. Here is mine. If you aren't familiar with any, do look them up. If you think I have missed something, let me know. :-)

  • Euler's identity
  • Everything about calculus
  • Principle of conservation of momentum
  • Gauss divergence theorem
  • Fourier series and Transforms, the FFT algorithm.
  • Eigen value analysis - natural vibrating frequencies of objects
  • Mohr's circle - How a piece of chalk breaks under tension and torsion
  • Gyroscopic effect and how bicycles work
  • Coriolis effect
  • Castigliano's theorems
  • Cauchy's integral theorem
  • Planck's black body radiation
  • Special relativity and general relativity
  • Michelson and Morley experiment - speed of light
  • Principle of equivalence
  • Young's double slit experiment
  • Superfluid helium
  • Cryogenic liquefaction cycles - how gases are liquified
  • Monte Carlo method
  • Modal superposition technique
  • Fermat's last theorem
  • Neural Networks
  • Absorption of sound in porous media
